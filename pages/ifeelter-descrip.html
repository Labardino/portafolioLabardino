The iFeelter Project



iFeelter is a concept of how we could link people’s feelings in a more intuitive way with social media, specifically the “stories” aspect.


Process description


The first aspect we had to define was user interaction. How would we make this easy and fun to use? We decided that the best way to interact with this plug-in would be with an artificial intelligence that could detect a person’s facial expressions along with some hand movements. Depending on the facial expressions done a specific emotion would appear. Whenever you put up your fist, you will stay in that specific emotion so you could see other filter offered for that emotion
We then wanted to make it easier to understand how the plug-in was functioning. What if I wanted to convey a certain emotion, but I did nor know how. This is why we decided to implement a way for the user to know which facial expressions it detected, with the possibility of knowing what other expressions it could detect.
These filters were created in Adobe Photoshop and passed a screening process through many polls made to college students. Afterwards, in order to put these backgrounds behind us we used a Green screen and edited in Adobe After Effects. We also decided to change the user’s overall hue in order to better convey the feeling. During this process we thought that the usage of gifs was a good idea. This added a more dynamic feeling to this smart plug-in.

Hardships

What I loved

What I learned

The fact that we had to create filters in order to convey certain feelings, was difficult. We had to do a painstakingly process of trial and error with all the filters.
I loved the fact of how we could always put in extra elements to our project, how after the trial and error we found the correct filter for an emotion. And I especially loved the end-product.
This was the first time I started to see how to manipulate a Green Screen with Adobe After Effects. It was the first time I thought of a new form of interaction
